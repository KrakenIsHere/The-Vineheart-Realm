# Dwarves

> [!QUOTE] Core concept
> Everything worth doing has **one true form**; to do it any other way is a lie told to the world.

## The perfect form
Dwarves inherited the old creed of [[Gods#Morlith, The Mountain Lord|Morlith]]: that every craft, every task, every stone has a single ideal it is meant to reach, and the worker's whole duty is to reach it. A shoddy weld or a "good enough" wall isn't merely poor work; it is a small **dishonesty**, a thing pretending to be what it is not.

They apprentice for decades and measure a life by what they mastered, not what they owned. A Dwarf will gladly spend three years on a task a Human would rush in a week, and consider the Human to have wasted the week.

## Mountain and hearth
Dwarves are the backbone of [[Onyxia|Onyxian]] mining and stonework, though their halls reach into every mountain range. **Mountain** dwarves pursue the perfect *work*: stone, steel, and craft. **Hill** dwarves pursue the perfect *hearth*: a home, a table, a community done right.

> [!TIP] Friction
> Others hear a Dwarf's flat "that is done wrong" as arrogance. To the Dwarf it is plain honesty, and staying quiet while you botched the thing would have been the real insult.
