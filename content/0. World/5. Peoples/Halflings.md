# Halflings

> [!QUOTE] Core concept
> **Luck** is shared, and it must be tended.

## The common luck
Halflings believe luck is real, and that it is not personal but *communal*: a shared pool that a family or a village holds together. You tend it with small courtesies and small rituals; you spend it with boasting, greed, and needless risk. A Halfling who gambles the household's fortune on pride hasn't only risked themselves; they've **stolen** from everyone.

In a realm where fortune does sometimes visibly bend in mortals' favour ([[DM Custom Rules#Luck Events|Luck Events]]), this reads less like superstition than like plain observation.

## Rooted and roaming
Halflings are the quiet heart of many [[Luminia|Luminian]] farm villages, and their two kindreds tend luck differently: **Stout** halflings keep the *rooted* luck of hearth and harvest, while **Lightfoot** halflings carry a *travelling* luck, sharing it road to road.

> [!TIP] Friction
> Others hear the constant small rituals and call Halflings superstitious. Halflings, in turn, quietly distrust the loud and the grasping, the sort who "spend the whole room's luck" and never think to put any back.
