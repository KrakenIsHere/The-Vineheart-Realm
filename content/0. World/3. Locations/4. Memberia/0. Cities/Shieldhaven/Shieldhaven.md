# Shieldhaven
Capital of [[Memberia]] and seat of [[Memberia#The House of Sapient|House Sapient]]: the oldest home of magical scholarship in the realm, grown outward from the first university ever raised on the continent. Its name is no accident. When the [[Divine War]] set the sky alight, the Sapient mages threw a vast protective ward over their city, and it became a *haven* for refugees, relics, and knowledge sheltered behind that *shield*. The wards still stand, and the city has guarded memory ever since.

## Notable Features
- The **Sapient Athenaeum**: the first and foremost magical university
- The **Memorium**: the largest archive of pre- and post-war knowledge in the realm
- The **Great Aegis**: the war-era ward that still cloaks the city
- Rule by the **Convocation**, where no vote is counted until three schools of magic are mastered

# Location
Shieldhaven rises inland at the heart of [[Memberia]], terraced up from the shore of cold, mirror-still **Glasswater Lake** and ringed by old-growth forest. Its towers are cut from pale frost-runed stone that sheds snow and holds warmth, so the city glitters through the long northern winter rather than freezing under it.

Three great roads leave its gates: south to [[Raven's Watch]], the port that carries Memberian trade to [[Arcanum]]; south-west to [[Shadowbrook]], the junction bound for [[Luminia]]; and north, toward the frozen coast and the temple of the **Frost Speaker**, where the [[Godwound Containment#Frost Tethering|Frost Tethering]] rite is kept.

# Governance
House Sapient rules not from a throne but from the **Convocation**, a council of magisters seated by mastery rather than birth. By Sapient tradition, no member may hold authority until they have mastered at least three schools of magic, and the house's own claim rests on scholarship as much as blood: their legitimacy descends maternally from the fallen royal house of **Mandia**, whose last heir they outlived through knowledge, not war.

The Convocation is chaired by the **Archmagister**, first among the magisters and the public face of the kingdom. Debate is endless, minuted, and archived; governing here looks a great deal like defending a thesis.

# The Athenaeum & the Memorium
The **Sapient Athenaeum** is the beating heart of the city and the reason it exists: students come from every continent to study where formal magic was first taught. Wrapped around it is the **Memorium**, a descending vault of libraries said to hold the fullest record of the world before and after the Godwound. The Godwounded keep a standing presence here: their **Memory Keepers** and **Wound Scribes** trade knowledge with the house, and help watch over the Memorium's sealed lower stacks, where pre-war divine texts too dangerous to read openly are quarantined.

# The Great Aegis
The ward that saved the city in the war never came down. The **Great Aegis** is anchored in a ring of ward-stones beneath the terraces, renewed on a cycle by the Convocation's finest abjurers. It turns aside storm, scrying, and the worst of the Godwound's weather, but it drains rare components faster each decade, and quiet arguments have begun over how long the realm can afford to keep one city so perfectly safe.

# Culture & Economy
Prestige in Shieldhaven is measured in knowledge. Enchanting, cartography, tutoring, and the copying and appraisal of texts drive its wealth, and a scholar's word can open doors that gold cannot. Examinations are civic events; a newly minted third-school magister is cause for a street festival. Outsiders find the city welcoming but exacting: it respects the curious and has little patience for the incurious.

> [!TIP] Adventure Hooks
> - A researcher vanishes into the Memorium's sealed stacks chasing a fragment of the [[The Immortal Soul|Immortal Soul]] legend, and the quarantine seals are found broken from the inside.
> - The Great Aegis is thinning. The Convocation needs rare warding components gathered before the next storm season, and cannot agree whether the cost is worth it.
> - An Archmagister's seat falls vacant, and a favored candidate is rumored to have mastered a *fourth*, forbidden school to win it.
> - Records in the Memorium are being quietly altered. Someone is not stealing history here; they are rewriting it.
