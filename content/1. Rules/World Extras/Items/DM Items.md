# DM Items

Secret clauses for the items over in [[Items]]. Players don't get to read this.

## Bag of Holding
Every single time someone pulls an item out of a Bag of Holding, roll a d100 where they can't see it. On a 100 the space inside hiccups, a seam of the [[The Godwound|Godwound]] snags on the pocket, and something slips out through the opening along with whatever they were reaching for.

> [!NOTE] Here's the thing though.
> In the Vineheart Realm the pocket inside a Bag isn't as stable as the old texts promise. Ever since the [[The Godwound|Godwound]], these little folds of elsewhere keep brushing up against places they shouldn't, and every so often something on the other side reaches back.

That's a flat 1% per retrieval, so a Bag that gets used all the time will go off eventually, usually at the worst possible moment. Bump the odds up whenever it feels right, a cursed or overstuffed Bag, or one hauled around too close to a Godwound manifestation (a 96-100 band makes it a 5% chance if you want it to actually bite).

When it does go off, roll a d10 for what comes through, or just pick the one that ruins their day best:

| d10 | What comes through                                                                                                        |
| --- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1-4 | A lesser Demon, confused and furious, right in the middle of the party.                                                   |
| 5-6 | A swarm of small nasties (imps, stirges, spiders) that scatter into the room.                                             |
| 7   | A gout of Godwound residue. Everyone close makes a CON save or eats a little necrotic damage and spends a round coughing. |
| 8   | Something that's already dead. A rotted arm, a half-formed thing, harmless but deeply wrong to look at.                   |
| 9   | A lost traveler or animal that fell into some other Bag somewhere, scared out of their wits and maybe grateful.           |
| 10  | Roll on your worst table. A proper Demon, or something with a Name, has noticed the door.                                 |

Either way the item still comes out too, so the player gets what they reached for. The Bag keeps working afterward like nothing happened, which is exactly why people keep pushing their luck with them.

## Ring of Three Wishes
The Ring is the honest one, so mostly play its Wishes straight. Even then, "roughly what you ask" still leaves you a little room, and how any Wish actually lands is its own can of worms, see [[DM Magical Events#Wishes|Wishes]].

## Luck Blade
The Wishes in the pommel work like any other, never as simple as they sound, see [[DM Magical Events#Wishes|Wishes]]. Roll the number of charges in secret (1d4) and don't tell them how many are left. Half the fun is watching them decide whether to gamble on one more.

## Efreeti Bottle
The Efreeti will Twist the wording of every Wish it grants if you leave it the smallest gap to work with. Word their Wishes back at them like you're the lawyer who hates them, see [[DM Magical Events#Wishes|Wishes]]. It wants out, and it wants whoever put it to work to regret the insult.

## Well of Many Worlds
Every so often the Well does something properly Wish-like instead of just opening a door, and never the way anyone wanted, see [[DM Magical Events#Wishes|Wishes]]. Opening it carelessly is asking for trouble in a realm where the [[The Godwound|Godwound]] already has the walls between places worn thin, so a careless unfold near a manifestation is all the excuse you need for something to come through the wrong way.
