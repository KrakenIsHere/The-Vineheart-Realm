# The Peoples of the Vineheart Realm

Hub for the **cultures and worldviews** of the realm's peoples: how they *think*, not how they fight.

> [!NOTE] Where cultural lore lives
> - **This area (`5. Peoples`)** covers how a people *sees the world*: its values, taboos, and the friction those create with everyone else.
> - **Race mechanics** (ability scores, traits, subraces) stay in [[Character Creation#Races|Character Creation]].
> - **Location culture** (how a specific city or continent lives) stays inside that place's own note.

## The Core Races
"**Core Races**" is the loose term for the peoples who rebuilt the realm's cities and kingdoms after the [[Divine War]]: chiefly humans, dwarves, elves, halflings, gnomes, and their kin. They share enough common ground (property, contracts, coin, the ordinary give-and-take of asking for help) that they mostly understand one another even when they disagree.

Their shared assumptions are so ingrained they rarely notice them. That is, until they meet a people who does not share them at all.

### Index
- [[Humans]]: a life is short, so it has to **count**
- [[Dwarves]]: everything worth doing has **one true form**
- [[Elves]]: the **past** is not gone; they walk it
- [[Gnomes]]: to stop **asking** is to start dying
- [[Halflings]]: **luck** is shared, and must be tended
- [[Half-Elves]]: **home** is a choice, not a birthright
- [[Half-Orcs]]: you are who **raised** you, not who bore you
- [[Dragonborn]]: what you **feel**, you show
- [[Tieflings]]: blood is a rumor; **deeds** are the truth

## The Others
Peoples whose worldview runs on entirely different rules. To the Core Races they can seem rude, greedy, or dangerous; to themselves, they are simply living *correctly*, and it is the Core Races who behave strangely.

### Index
- [[Goblins]]: a people with no concept of **Property**
- [[Orcs]]: a people who hold that the **Soul** grows only through **Challenge**

*(Both lists will grow as more cultures are written; the [[Goblins]] and [[Orcs]] are only the first of the Others.)*
